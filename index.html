Documentation: How BwE Works

This application demonstrates the Bandwidth Enforcer (BwE) model used by Google to manage its private Wide Area Network (WAN). Unlike simple fairness, BwE uses hierarchical, priority-aware policies to ensure critical services never drop below their minimum needs.

1. Multi-Region Priority Functions

In BwE, each service defines its demand as a Bandwidth Function. Instead of a single priority, a service like YouTube has two regions:

  • Guaranteed (Red): The minimum bandwidth required for basic functionality. Allocated at Priority 10.
  • Best-Effort (Green): Extra bandwidth for higher quality or pre-fetching. Allocated at Priority 1.

2. Priority-Aware Water-filling

The allocation engine runs in two distinct stages to maximize utility rather than just bits:

  1. Stage 1: It attempts to fulfill the Red demands for every service using standard Max-Min fairness.
  2. Stage 2: If network capacity remains, it moves to the Green demands. If the capacity is reached here, the Best-Effort traffic is capped equally across all services.

3. Demand Estimation

BwE relies on Bottom-Up Demand Estimation. Each host reports not just what it is currently sending, but what it could send if the pipe were wider. This is calculated as:
Estimated Demand = Current Throughput + Local Queue Size.
